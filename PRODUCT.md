# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Everyday New Zealanders buying a vehicle — salaried and wage-earning car buyers who
need finance to drive away. They are not finance experts. They arrive anxious about
two things: *"Will I get approved?"* and *"What will this actually cost me each week?"*
Many are comparing a handful of lenders/brokers on their phone, often after being
quoted by a dealer. They want a straight answer, a sense of the real cost, and a
human they can trust — not jargon, not a hard sell.

## Product Purpose

Northwest Finance is a New Zealand vehicle-finance company. The website exists to:

1. Establish trust quickly with a first-time visitor who has never heard of the brand.
2. Explain plainly what Northwest does and who it can help.
3. Let visitors estimate their weekly/fortnightly repayments themselves (calculator),
   removing the biggest source of uncertainty before they make contact.
4. Convert that confidence into an enquiry (call / contact).

Success = a visitor understands the offer, trusts the brand, estimates a repayment,
and reaches out — without ever feeling pressured or confused.

## Brand Personality

Modern and approachable, but grounded and credible. **Three words: clear, steady,
human.** The voice is plain-spoken and reassuring — a knowledgeable friend who works
in finance, not a bank's legal department and not a pushy salesperson. Warm without
being cute; confident without being corporate. Emotional goal: *relief and
confidence* — "these people make this simple, and I can trust them with something
that matters."

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI landing pages**: gradient-text headings, identical icon-card grids,
  a tiny tracked uppercase eyebrow above every section, hero-metric template, faux
  glassmorphism. The brief explicitly rejects this look.
- **Predatory / payday-loan aesthetics**: urgent red, "APPROVED IN 60 SECONDS!!!"
  shouting, countdown timers, aggressive yellow CTAs.
- **Cold enterprise-bank corporate**: stock photos of suited handshakes, navy-and-gold
  cliché, dense legalese, impersonal stock photography.
- **Visually busy / overdesigned**: too many competing elements, decorative noise.
  The user wants calm and focused — one clear idea per section.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust on sight.** Every screen should read as a real, established NZ
   business — credible, specific, and local. Specificity (real numbers, real process,
   real contact) beats adjectives.
2. **Answer the money question early.** Cost and approval are the visitor's anxieties;
   surface the calculator and clear "how it works" prominently rather than burying them.
3. **Calm over clever.** One dominant idea per section, generous space, restrained
   palette. Remove anything that doesn't help the visitor decide.
4. **Plain human language.** No jargon, no fine-print voice in headlines. Say what we
   mean the way a person would say it out loud.
5. **Honest, not hypey.** Reassure without overpromising; finance is regulated and
   trust is fragile. Confidence comes from clarity, not exclamation marks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥ 3:1. Full keyboard
operability for the nav and the calculator; visible focus states. Calculator inputs
must be usable by keyboard and screen reader (labelled sliders + number inputs,
announced results). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Don't rely on color alone to
convey meaning. Audience skews non-expert and all ages — legibility and clear labels
matter more than density.
